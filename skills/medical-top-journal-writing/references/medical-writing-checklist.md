# Medical Writing Checklist

## Contents

- Universal manuscript checks
- Methods checks
- Results checks
- Discussion checks
- Submission package checks
- AI and authorship checks

## Universal manuscript checks

- Match the article type, study design, title, abstract, and subtitle.
- Keep the clinical importance explicit from the first paragraph onward.
- Ensure every numerical claim can be traced to a source table, figure, or dataset note.
- Make terminology consistent for exposures, interventions, outcomes, and time points.

## Methods checks

- State study design, setting, location or system, study period, and follow-up window.
- Define eligibility, exclusions, recruitment or sampling, and final analytic sample.
- Define intervention or exposure precisely.
- Pre-specify primary and major secondary outcomes.
- Describe statistical methods, confounding control, missing data handling, multiplicity handling, and software where relevant.
- Include ethics approval, consent language, and trial registration when applicable.
- For trials, note protocol and SAP availability.

## Results checks

- Start with participant flow and baseline characteristics.
- Present the primary outcome before secondary, subgroup, or exploratory analyses.
- Report absolute numbers and denominators where possible.
- Pair P values with effect estimates and confidence intervals.
- Report harms, adverse events, missingness, and protocol deviations if relevant.
- Keep abstract numbers and manuscript numbers perfectly aligned.

## Discussion checks

- Start with the principal findings, not a literature review.
- Compare with prior evidence only after stating what the study found.
- Make limitations explicit, concrete, and proportionate.
- Translate findings into clinical or policy relevance without overclaiming.
- For observational work, prefer "was associated with" over causal verbs unless justified.

## Submission package checks

- cover letter
- title page and author metadata
- funding and role of sponsor statement
- conflict of interest disclosures
- contributor roles and acknowledgments
- data sharing statement if required
- reporting-guideline checklist and flow diagram
- protocol, SAP, survey instrument, or supplement if the journal expects them
- confirmation that related manuscripts, prior postings, or prior reviews have been disclosed if relevant

## AI and authorship checks

Apply ICMJE-oriented rules:

- Human authors must meet authorship criteria and remain accountable for the work.
- Do not list AI systems as authors.
- If AI-assisted technology materially contributed to writing assistance, disclose that in the acknowledgment section and also mention it in the cover letter when the journal requires it.
- If AI was used for analysis, coding, image generation, or figure preparation, describe that use in Methods or another appropriate manuscript section.
- Review every AI-assisted output for error, bias, missing attribution, and unsupported claims before presenting it as manuscript text.
